Time and Place

77 Massachusetts Avenue, MIT Building 1, Room 1-190. Be there by 9 AM.

Cost

The registration fee is (minimum) $5. All proceeds will go to Action in Africa.

Schedule

The contest will start with three timed individual rounds. All problems will test problem-solving skills, with most concepts taken from the fundamental AP Calculus AB curriculum, although some BC concepts may help. The problems will be simple in spirit, but tough to solve!

  • The first round will consist of 5 integration problems and will last 12 minutes.
  • The second round will consist of ten general short-answer problems, and will last 42 minutes.
  • The third round will consist of five free-response problems and will last 30 minutes.

There will then be a 10-problem, 20-minute team round. To do well on the team round, you should try to fill your team to 5 members.

After the team round, we will provide free lunch to all participants. (Our food sponsors are not finalized yet, but we assure you that there will be awesome food!)

After food, there will shorter team relays, in which each problem's answer plugs into the next problem's statement.

While we grade all the contests, we will host a very short calculus puzzle hunt and the winning team will get a secret prize. Finally, there will be a talk by a guest speaker after which we conclude the day with the awards ceremony.

(An exact timed schedule will be available closer to the contest.)

About

CalcBee is a live calculus competition organized and run by high school students.

We're affiliated with the Online Science Olympiads. If you're interested in physics, chemistry, or computer science, you should go check out their competitions!
